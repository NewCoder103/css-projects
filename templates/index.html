Why to upgrade to Linux

By Arnaud Alzar

We all know the pain of when Windows decides to update when you want to shut it down. However, with Linux, you dictate when you want to update.
But, these updates are not triggered on shutdown. Instead, we update using the system's package manager, APT.

Why is this important? Well, Linux's ecosystem doesn't simply function off of proprietary software, but instead, by open-source.
Linux works thanks to the power of open-sourced software, and thanks to that, we don't need to always restart when an update is required.

Well, why not Windows 10 or 11? Well, if you don't stay in the loop, they are discontinuing Win10, in favor of Windows 11.
Last time, they promised us that Windows 10 will be the final OS, and they broke that promise.

With Linux, you can control when you want to switch. Lightweight linux distributions give you control on what you want to install.
Linux gaming is also slowly but surely increasing, with a lot of support from the community and Valve especially.

My favorite Linux distribution is Linux Mint. It's a lightweight distribution that has the look and feel of normal Windows 10.
